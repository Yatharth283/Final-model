Frequently Asked Questions - Metals and Non-Metals

1. Define corrosion. What is the specific term for the corrosion of iron? Why is it considered a serious problem, and how can it be prevented?

Corrosion is the gradual destruction of metals due to reactions with environmental factors such as moisture and air. The corrosion of iron is specifically termed rusting. It weakens iron structures, causing safety hazards and economic losses. It can be prevented by painting, galvanization, oiling, and electroplating.

2. Explain the process and conditions under which iron rusts.

Iron rusts when exposed to both oxygen and water. The reaction forms hydrated iron(III) oxide (Fe₂O₃·xH₂O), commonly known as rust. This process accelerates in the presence of electrolytes like salt.

3. Why do some metal surfaces acquire a dull appearance when exposed to moist air?

Metals react with oxygen and moisture in the air, forming oxides or other compounds that cause a dull appearance. For example, copper forms a greenish layer of copper carbonate, and silver forms a black coating of silver sulfide.

4. Describe an activity to demonstrate the conditions necessary for iron to rust.

Take three test tubes:

  • Test tube A: Iron nails in water → rusts
  • Test tube B: Iron nails in boiled water with oil → does not rust
  • Test tube C: Dry iron nails → does not rust

This shows that both oxygen and water are needed for rusting.

5. A metal 'X' combines with a non-metal 'Y' by transferring electrons to form compound 'Z'.

  • (i) Type of bond in compound Z: Ionic bond
  • (ii) Melting & boiling points: High due to strong electrostatic forces
  • (iii) Solubility in kerosene/petrol: Insoluble (ionic compounds dissolve in water, not in non-polar solvents)
  • (iv) Electrical conductivity: Conducts electricity in molten/aqueous state but not in solid form

6. Differentiate between metals and non-metals based on their chemical properties.

  • Metals: Lose electrons, form basic oxides, react with acids to produce hydrogen gas.
  • Non-Metals: Gain electrons, form acidic oxides, do not react with acids.

7. What are amphoteric oxides? Identify them among Na₂O, ZnO, CO₂, Al₂O₃, H₂O.

Amphoteric oxides react with both acids and bases. Among the given options, ZnO (Zinc Oxide) and Al₂O₃ (Aluminum Oxide) are amphoteric.

8. Why is copper used to make hot water tanks instead of steel?

Copper is less reactive than iron and does not react with hot water, preventing rusting and ensuring durability. Steel (iron alloy) can rust when exposed to hot water.
