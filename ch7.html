Control and Coordination

Introduction

Control and coordination in organisms help regulate various functions through the nervous and endocrine systems.

The Nervous System

Neuron Structure and Function

The neuron is the fundamental unit of the nervous system, responsible for transmitting signals.

  • Dendrites: Receive signals from other neurons.
  • Axon: Transmits signals to muscles or other neurons.
  • Synapse: Gap between neurons for signal transmission.

Types of Neurons

  • Sensory Neurons: Carry signals from sensory organs to the brain and spinal cord.
  • Motor Neurons: Send signals from the brain and spinal cord to muscles and glands.
  • Interneurons: Connect sensory and motor neurons within the central nervous system.

Human Nervous System

  • Central Nervous System (CNS): Comprises the brain and spinal cord.
  • Peripheral Nervous System (PNS): Consists of sensory and motor nerves.
  • Autonomic Nervous System (ANS): Controls involuntary functions like heartbeat and digestion.

Reflex Action

Reflex action is an automatic, rapid response to stimuli, protecting the body from harm.

  • Reflex Arc: The pathway followed by nerve impulses during reflex actions.

Brain Structure and Functions

  • Cerebrum: Controls voluntary actions, intelligence, memory, and reasoning.
  • Cerebellum: Maintains balance and posture.
  • Medulla Oblongata: Regulates involuntary actions like breathing and heartbeat.

Coordination in Plants

Plant Hormones

Hormone Function
Auxin Promotes stem elongation and root growth.
Cytokinin Encourages cell division and delays aging in leaves.
Gibberellins Stimulates stem growth, seed germination, and flowering.
Abscisic Acid Induces seed dormancy and closes stomata during stress.
Ethylene Promotes fruit ripening and leaf fall.

Types of Plant Movements

  • Tropic Movements: Growth movements in response to stimuli like light (phototropism) and gravity (gravitropism).
  • Nastic Movements: Non-directional responses to stimuli, such as the folding of Mimosa leaves upon touch.

Endocrine System

Hormonal Coordination in Humans

  • Pituitary Gland: The master gland that regulates growth, metabolism, and reproductive functions.
  • Thyroid Gland: Produces thyroxine, which controls metabolism.
  • Pancreas: Secretes insulin and glucagon to regulate blood sugar levels.
  • Adrenal Glands: Release adrenaline to prepare the body for emergency situations.
  • Gonads (Ovaries and Testes): Produce reproductive hormones like estrogen and testosterone.

Disorders Related to Hormonal Imbalance

  • Diabetes: Caused by a lack of insulin production from the pancreas.
  • Hyperthyroidism: Excessive production of thyroxine leading to increased metabolism.
  • Hypothyroidism: Deficiency of thyroxine leading to reduced metabolism and weight gain.
