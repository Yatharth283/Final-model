Class 10 Science - Light: Reflection & Refraction

Introduction

Light propagates in a straight line and exhibits various properties like reflection and refraction. This chapter explores these phenomena and their applications.

Nature of Light

  • Ray Nature: Light travels in straight lines, explaining reflection and refraction.
  • Wave Nature: Light exhibits interference and diffraction.
  • Particle Nature: Light behaves as a stream of particles in the photoelectric effect.
  • Dual Nature: Light exhibits both wave and particle behavior.

Laws of Reflection

  1. The incident ray, the reflected ray, and the normal lie in the same plane.
  2. The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.

Types of Reflection

Regular Reflection: Occurs on smooth surfaces like mirrors.

Diffuse Reflection: Occurs on rough surfaces, causing light to scatter.

Spherical Mirrors

Concave Mirror: Inward curved mirror used in torches, telescopes, and solar cookers.

Convex Mirror: Outward curved mirror used in vehicle rear-view mirrors.

Mirror Formula

1/v + 1/u = 1/f, where v = image distance, u = object distance, and f = focal length.

Refraction of Light

Refraction is the bending of light when it passes from one medium to another due to a change in speed.

Refractive Index

The ratio of the speed of light in vacuum to its speed in the given medium.

Lenses

Convex Lens: Converging lens, used in magnifying glasses and cameras.

Concave Lens: Diverging lens, used in correcting myopia.

Lens Formula: 1/v - 1/u = 1/f

Power of Lens: P = 1/f (in meters), measured in Dioptres (D).

Applications

  • Concave Mirrors: Used in torches, telescopes, and solar cookers.
  • Convex Mirrors: Used as security and vehicle rear-view mirrors.
  • Convex Lenses: Used in magnifying glasses and cameras.
  • Concave Lenses: Used in correcting myopia.

Dispersion of Light

When white light passes through a prism, it splits into its component colors (VIBGYOR).

Human Eye & Defects of Vision

Myopia (Nearsightedness): Corrected using concave lenses.

Hypermetropia (Farsightedness): Corrected using convex lenses.

Presbyopia: Age-related loss of vision corrected using bifocal lenses.

Prism & Atmospheric Refraction

Phenomena like twinkling of stars and advanced sunrise/sunset occur due to atmospheric refraction.

Scattering of Light

Shorter wavelengths scatter more than longer wavelengths. This explains why the sky appears blue and sunsets appear red.
