Frequently Asked Questions - Acids, Bases, and Salts

Important Questions

1. Reactions of Acids with Metals

Question: Describe the reaction between acids and active metals. How can the evolved gas be tested?

Answer: Acids react with active metals to produce a salt and hydrogen gas. The presence of hydrogen gas can be confirmed by bringing a burning matchstick near it; a 'pop' sound indicates hydrogen.

2. Acid-Base Indicators

Question: What are acid-base indicators? Provide examples.

Answer: Acid-base indicators are substances that change color in acidic or basic solutions. Examples include litmus, phenolphthalein, and methyl orange.

3. Neutralization Reactions

Question: Explain the process and significance of neutralization reactions.

Answer: Neutralization is the reaction between an acid and a base to form salt and water, releasing heat. This reaction is utilized in various applications, such as treating indigestion with antacids.

4. pH Scale and Its Applications

Question: What is the pH scale? How is it significant in daily life?

Answer: The pH scale measures the acidity or basicity of a solution, ranging from 0 to 14. A pH less than 7 indicates acidity, while a pH greater than 7 indicates basicity. The pH level is crucial in areas like agriculture, medicine, and environmental science.

5. Properties and Uses of Important Compounds

Question: Discuss the properties and uses of compounds such as baking soda, washing soda, and bleaching powder.

Answer: Baking soda is used in baking and as an antacid. Washing soda is employed in cleaning and water softening. Bleaching powder serves as a disinfectant and bleaching agent.

6. Water of Crystallization

Question: Define water of crystallization with examples.

Answer: Water of crystallization refers to the fixed number of water molecules present in a crystalline compound. For instance, gypsum (CaSO₄·2H₂O) contains two water molecules.

7. Plaster of Paris

Question: What is Plaster of Paris? How is it prepared, and what are its uses?

Answer: Plaster of Paris (CaSO₄·½H₂O) is produced by heating gypsum at 373 K. It is utilized in making molds, sculptures, and for setting fractured bones.

8. Effect of Acid Rain

Question: What is acid rain, and what are its effects on the environment?

Answer: Acid rain contains elevated levels of acidic components, such as sulfuric and nitric acids. It can harm aquatic life, damage vegetation, and erode buildings and monuments.

9. Common Salt and Its Derivatives

Question: Explain the importance of common salt and its derivatives.

Answer: Common salt (sodium chloride) is essential in the diet and serves as a raw material for products like baking soda, washing soda, and bleaching powder.

10. pH and Tooth Decay

Question: How does pH relate to tooth decay?

Answer: Tooth enamel can erode when the pH in the mouth falls below 5.5 due to acidic food and bacterial activity, leading to cavities.
