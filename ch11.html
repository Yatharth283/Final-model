Chapter 11 - The Human Eye and the Colourful World

Structure of a Human Eye

The human eye is a spherical organ with an average diameter of 2.3 cm. It enables vision by forming images on the retina, a light-sensitive screen.

  • Cornea: Transparent membrane that allows light to enter. Most of the light refraction occurs here.
  • Iris: Muscular diaphragm that controls pupil size.
  • Pupil: Regulates the amount of light entering the eye.
  • Lens: Focuses light onto the retina.
  • Ciliary Muscles: Adjust the lens shape for focusing.
  • Retina: Light-sensitive layer where images are formed.
  • Optic Nerve: Sends visual signals to the brain.
  • Aqueous and Vitreous Humor: Provide shape and nourishment to the eye.
  • Suspensory Ligament: Holds the lens in place.

Defects of a Human Eye

  • Myopia (Short-sightedness): Nearby objects are clear, distant ones are blurry. Corrected with a concave lens.
  • Hypermetropia (Far-sightedness): Distant objects are clear, nearby ones are blurry. Corrected with a convex lens.
  • Presbyopia: Age-related condition causing difficulty in focusing on nearby objects. Corrected using bifocal lenses.
  • Cataract: Cloudy lens due to protein deposition. Treated by replacing the lens.

Dispersion of White Light by a Glass Prism

A prism splits white light into seven colors (VIBGYOR) due to different refractive indices of light colors. Red bends the least, violet bends the most.

Atmospheric Refraction

Twinkling of Stars

Starlight continuously refracts through different atmospheric layers, causing the star to appear twinkling.

Why Planets Do Not Twinkle

Planets appear larger and emit light from multiple points, reducing the effect of atmospheric refraction.

Advanced Optical Effects

Atmospheric refraction causes mirages and other optical illusions due to temperature-induced density variations.

Scattering of Light

Light interacts with particles in a medium, causing some rays to scatter. The extent depends on particle size and wavelength.

The Tyndall Effect

The scattering of light by particles in a colloidal solution, responsible for the blue sky and red sunsets.
