Frequently Asked Questions - Life Processes

1. What are life processes? Name the essential life processes in living organisms.

Life processes are the biological functions necessary for the survival of living organisms. The essential life processes include:

  • Nutrition - Obtaining food and energy.
  • Respiration - Breaking down food to release energy.
  • Transportation - Movement of nutrients and oxygen.
  • Excretion - Removal of metabolic waste.

3. Differentiate between aerobic and anaerobic respiration.

Aerobic Respiration: Occurs in the presence of oxygen, producing more energy. Anaerobic Respiration: Occurs without oxygen, producing less energy (e.g., fermentation in yeast).

4. How does the human circulatory system function?

The human circulatory system consists of the heart, blood, and blood vessels. The heart pumps oxygenated blood through arteries and receives deoxygenated blood through veins.

5. Describe the human excretory system.

The human excretory system consists of **kidneys, ureters, urinary bladder, and urethra**. The kidneys filter blood to remove waste, forming urine, which is stored in the bladder and excreted through the urethra.

6. What is the role of stomata in plants?

Stomata are small openings on plant leaves that control gas exchange and transpiration. They allow CO2 to enter for photosynthesis and release oxygen.
