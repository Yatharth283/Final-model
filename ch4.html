Carbon and its Compounds

Chapter 4: Carbon and its Compounds

Carbon is one of the most important elements found on Earth. It is the backbone of all living organisms and forms a vast number of compounds due to its unique properties.

1. Introduction to Carbon

Carbon is a non-metal with the symbol C and atomic number 6. It is present in various forms like diamonds, graphite, and coal.

Why is Carbon Special?

  • It forms strong covalent bonds with other elements.
  • It can form long chains and rings (a property called catenation).
  • It has four valence electrons, allowing it to bond with four other atoms.

2. Covalent Bonding in Carbon

Carbon forms covalent bonds by sharing electrons with other atoms.

Types of Covalent Bonds:

  • Single Bond: One pair of shared electrons (e.g., Methane - CH₄).
  • Double Bond: Two pairs of shared electrons (e.g., Ethene - C₂H₄).
  • Triple Bond: Three pairs of shared electrons (e.g., Ethyne - C₂H₂).

3. Allotropes of Carbon

Carbon exists in different physical forms called allotropes.

Types of Allotropes:

  • Diamond: Hardest substance, does not conduct electricity.
  • Graphite: Soft, slippery, and conducts electricity.
  • Fullerenes: Spherical molecules of carbon (e.g., C₆₀ - Buckminsterfullerene).

4. Hydrocarbons

Compounds made of only carbon and hydrogen are called hydrocarbons.

Types of Hydrocarbons:

  • Saturated Hydrocarbons (Alkanes): Have single bonds (e.g., Methane - CH₄).
  • Unsaturated Hydrocarbons (Alkenes & Alkynes): Have double or triple bonds (e.g., Ethene - C₂H₄, Ethyne - C₂H₂).

5. Functional Groups

Atoms or groups of atoms attached to a carbon chain that determine the chemical properties of a compound.

Common Functional Groups:

  • -OH (Alcohols): Found in ethanol, methanol.
  • -COOH (Carboxylic Acids): Found in vinegar (acetic acid).
  • -CHO (Aldehydes): Found in formaldehyde.
  • -C=O (Ketones): Found in propanone (acetone).

6. Important Carbon Compounds

Carbon compounds are used in daily life.

Examples:

  • Ethanol (Alcohol): Used in sanitizers, alcoholic drinks.
  • Acetic Acid (Vinegar): Used in food preservatives.
  • Soap and Detergents: Used for cleaning.
  • Plastics and Polymers: Used in bags, bottles.

7. Chemical Reactions of Carbon Compounds

Carbon compounds undergo various reactions.

Important Reactions:

  • Combustion: Carbon compounds burn in oxygen to release CO₂, water, and heat.
  • Oxidation: Alcohols get oxidized to acids (Ethanol → Acetic acid).
  • Addition Reaction: Hydrogen is added to unsaturated hydrocarbons in the presence of a catalyst (Used in vegetable oil hydrogenation).
  • Substitution Reaction: Hydrogen atoms in alkanes are replaced with other atoms (e.g., chlorine in methane).

8. Applications of Carbon Compounds

Carbon compounds have huge industrial applications.

Uses:

  • Medicine: Used in drugs like aspirin.
  • Fuel: Petrol, diesel, and natural gas are hydrocarbons.
  • Food Industry: Carbohydrates and fats are carbon-based.
  • Plastic & Rubber: Used in daily life items.
