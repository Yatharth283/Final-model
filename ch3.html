Metals and Non-Metals

Introduction

Elements are broadly classified into Metals and Non-Metals based on their properties.

Physical Properties of Metals

  • Malleability: Can be beaten into thin sheets (e.g., Gold, Silver).
  • Ductility: Can be drawn into wires (e.g., Copper, Aluminum).
  • Conductivity: Good conductors of heat and electricity.
  • Luster: Have a shiny appearance.
  • Sonority: Produce ringing sound when struck.
  • High Melting & Boiling Points: Except Mercury, which is liquid at room temperature.

Physical Properties of Non-Metals

  • Brittle: Break easily (e.g., Sulfur, Phosphorus).
  • Poor Conductors: Except Graphite.
  • Dull Appearance: Except Iodine.
  • Low Density: Generally lighter than metals.

Chemical Properties of Metals

  • Reaction with Oxygen: Metals form basic oxides.
  • Example: \( 4Na + O_2 \rightarrow 2Na_2O \) (Sodium Oxide).
  • Reaction with Water: Metals form hydroxides and hydrogen gas.
  • Example: \( 2Na + 2H_2O \rightarrow 2NaOH + H_2 \).
  • Reaction with Acids: Metals form salts and hydrogen gas.
  • Example: \( Zn + 2HCl \rightarrow ZnCl_2 + H_2 \).

Chemical Properties of Non-Metals

  • Reaction with Oxygen: Forms acidic oxides.
  • Example: \( C + O_2 \rightarrow CO_2 \).
  • No Reaction with Water: Non-metals do not react with water.

Reactivity Series of Metals

The **reactivity series** arranges metals based on their reactivity:

Highly Reactive: K, Na, Ca

Moderately Reactive: Mg, Al, Zn, Fe

Least Reactive: Cu, Ag, Au

Corrosion of Metals

Corrosion is the slow destruction of metals due to reactions with the environment.

Example: Rusting of Iron

\( Fe + O_2 + H_2O \rightarrow Fe_2O_3 \cdot xH_2O \) (Rust)

Prevention:

  • Galvanization (zinc coating)
  • Painting/Oiling
  • Electroplating

Uses of Metals

  • Iron: Construction, machinery, tools.
  • Copper: Electrical wiring, coins.
  • Aluminum: Aircraft, utensils.
  • Gold & Silver: Jewelry, electronics.

Uses of Non-Metals

  • Oxygen: Respiration, combustion.
  • Carbon: Fuel, steel-making.
  • Nitrogen: Fertilizers, industry.
  • Phosphorus: Fireworks, fertilizers.

Conclusion

Metals and non-metals have distinct properties, making them useful in different applications.
